            <h2>Introduction</h2>
            <p>Arm wrestling is a highly competitive sport that requires a combination of muscular strength, strategic
                leverage, and precise hand positioning. To win a match, pulling athletes must master the core technical
                styles rather than relying solely on raw power.</p>
        </section>

        <section>
            <h2>3 Most Common Arm Wrestling Styles</h2>
            <p>Below is a list of the three fundamental techniques used by professional pullers on the table:</p>

            <ul>
                <li><strong>Toproll:</strong> An outside move focused on leverage. The puller applies pressure to the
                    opponent's fingers, forcing their hand open and breaking their wrist flexion.</li>
                <li><strong>Hook:</strong> An inside power move. The puller curls their wrist inward, engaging the
                    biceps and forearm muscles to engage in a close-quarters strength battle.</li>
                <li><strong>Press:</strong> A finishing move where the puller rotates their shoulder forward, using
                    upper body weight and triceps strength to drive the opponent's hand down straight to the pad.</li>
            </ul>
        </section>
